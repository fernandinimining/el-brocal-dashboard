Disclosures & Assumptions — Financial Overview

  • Data source: All financial data from El Brocal audited annual financial statements (EEFF) filed with SMV Peru, 2018-2025. Revenue data available from 2019.
  • 2026E projections: Based on BVN guidance (48-53 kMT Cu), Q1 2026 LME copper price of $5.82/lb, and 2025 cost structure held constant in real terms. EBITDA and net income assume no extraordinary items.
  • Cash projection (post-dividend): Assumes 50% of 2026E net income ($265M) distributed as dividends = $132.5M. Pre-dividend cash = $173M + $265M - $6M capex est. = $432M. Post-dividend = $300M.
  • EBITDA: Calculated as Operating Income + D&A. Not directly reported in EEFF; derived from Note 17 and income statement.
  • Currency: All values in US dollars (thousands or millions as labeled). El Brocal reports in soles; figures converted at annual average exchange rates per BCRP.

Blending strategy potential: By sourcing ~30% clean concentrate for blending, El Brocal could reduce effective arsenic to 2-3%, improving payability from 86.3% to ~91%. At 2026E revenue of $729M, a 5pp payability improvement = ~$36M incremental revenue. Net of blending costs (~$10-15M), annual upside is $15-25M. This strategy was not pursued by BVN management despite clear precedent (Antamina blending program).
  • Breakeven peaked at $3.92/lb (2022) — just $0.08/lb below market price

  • Production cost per tonne of Cu: +1% since 2019 — no operational improvement

Disclosures & Assumptions — Production & Commercial

  • Production data: BVN SEC Form 6-K quarterly filings and 20-F annual reports. 2026E based on BVN guidance midpoint.
  • Metal prices: LME Cu spot annual averages (IMF/FRED). Silver and Gold from LBMA/FRED. 2026E uses Q1 2026 spot prices.
  • TC/RC: Annual benchmark terms from CRU/Fastmarkets. 2025 = $21.25/DMT and 2.125c/lb RC. 2026 = $0 benchmark (negative territory, settled at zero floor).
  • Concentrate quality: Arsenic content (7.5-8%), Cu grade (25%), and payability (86.3%) from SLR Technical Report (2023) and BVN disclosures.
  • Blending estimate: Illustrative. Assumes 30% clean concentrate blend, $10-15M sourcing cost. Actual economics depend on logistics, counterparty terms, and feed grade variability.
  • Breakeven calculation: Operating breakeven = (Total operating costs) / (Cu equivalent production in lbs). Includes all COGS + SGA.
  • GMV (Gross Metal Value): Production volume × average annual price per metal. Does not net out TC/RC or penalties.

Disclosures & Assumptions — Related-Party Transactions

  • Data source: El Brocal EEFF Note 25 (related-party transactions) and Note 17 (cost of production). All figures audited.
  • BVN ownership: Buenaventura (BVN) holds 61.43% of El Brocal. BVN is both majority shareholder and service provider.
  • Huanza: BVN subsidiary that was sole electricity supplier to El Brocal via PPA until 2024. In 2024, electricity cost dropped 54% after contract renegotiation/termination. "Fair market" benchmark = OSINERGMIN regulated tariff for comparable industrial consumers.
  • Insurance benchmark: Uses 2018 insurance-to-production-cost ratio (1.85%) as baseline. Excess = actual insurance minus (production cost × 1.85%). Contacto Corredores de Seguros (insurance broker) is a disclosed related party in Note 25. The insurer itself is not identified as a related party. Not a formal arm’s-length test — illustrative benchmark only.
  • Cumulative excess: Huanza electricity premium over benchmark (~$40M, 2018-2024) + insurance excess over 2018 baseline (~$22M, 2019-2025) = ~$62M in charges above benchmarks through these two channels. BVN management fees ($58M, 2018-2025) are separate and represent contracted services — not treated as excess. Formal arm’s-length pricing analysis required for definitive conclusions.
  • PPA contract: The Huanza power purchase agreement terms are not publicly disclosed. Analysis based on booked costs vs. regulated market benchmarks.
